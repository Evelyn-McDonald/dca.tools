What is DCA, and what inspired this tool?

Generally, dollar-cost averaging (DCA) is an investment technique of buying a fixed amount of a particular investment on a regular schedule, regardless of the share price. The investor purchases more shares when prices are low and fewer shares when prices are high.

However, the same concept could also be used to recover from losses in a shorter timeframe, by adding funds and lowering the average price of purchase. For example if you bought 1 Bitcoin at $20.000 and now the price is $5.000, you can lower the average cost of your investment, hence decreasing your loss, by buying 2 more Bitcoins. Now you have a total of 3 Bitcoins and you paid an average price of $10.000. This means that if your loss was -75% before your second purchase, it's now 50%. Not too shabby.
