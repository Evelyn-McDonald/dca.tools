What is DCA, and what inspired this tool?

Generally, dollar-cost averaging (DCA) is the investment technique of buying a fixed amount of a particular investment on a regular schedule, regardless of the its unit price. The investor purchases more units when prices are low and fewer units when prices are high.

However, this same concept can also be used to recover from losses in a shorter timeframe. By adding funds at rate lower than the one of your previous investment, you can lower your average cost, therefore greatly reducing your losses. You can achieve this either manually or with the aid of trading bots, which now offer various recovery strategies, including automatic DCA.

What about a practical example?

Sure! Say you bought 1 Bitcoin at $20.000 and now the price is $5.000. You got rekt, but all is not lost, you can lower the average cost of your investment, hence decreasing your loss, by buying 4 more Bitcoins at the current rate of $5.000. Now you have a total of 5 Bitcoins and you paid an average price of $8.000. This means that if your loss was -75% before your second purchase, it's now -37.5%. Not too shabby.
